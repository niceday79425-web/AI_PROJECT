Dividend Investing FAQ

Is a high dividend yield always good?

No, beware of the "Dividend Trap." An unusually high yield often indicates a dropping stock price due to underlying business issues. Always check the Payout Ratio (is it under 100%?) and the company's history of dividend cuts before buying.

Why invest in Dividend Growth stocks over High Yield?

To beat inflation. "Dividend Growth" stocks (e.g., Dividend Aristocrats) typically raise their payouts every year. While their starting yield might be lower, the Yield on Cost can become massive over 10+ years, protecting your purchasing power against inflation.

What is DRIP?

DRIP stands for Dividend Reinvestment Plan. It automatically uses your cash dividends to buy more partial shares of the stock. Its the key engine behind the "Snowball Effect," making compounding easy.

How are dividends taxed? (Important)

It depends on the type of dividend and where you hold it:

Qualified Dividends: Taxed at the lower long-term capital gains rate (0%, 15%, or 20%) if held for more than 60 days.

Ordinary Dividends (REITs): Taxed as regular income. Many investors hold REITs in tax-advantaged accounts like a Roth IRA to avoid this tax bill.

Why are REIT dividends so high?

It's because they are legally required to distribute 90% of their taxable income to shareholders. This allows them to avoid corporate-level taxes, but requires they pay out most of their cash flow to you.
