Industrial Stocks: HON, UPS, CAT

This sector forms the foundation of real economic activities—such as logistics, manufacturing, and construction machinery—and has grown its dividends based on long-term business know-how.

💡 Key Point: Although it is influenced by economic cycles, it provides solid dividends by benefiting from long-term trends like national infrastructure rebuilding and factory automation.

1. Correlation with Economic Cycles and Dividend Moat

Even if there is a temporary drop in orders during recessions, the sector operates stably without dividend cuts, thanks to its historically low dividend payout ratios.

2. Core Representative Stocks in the Industrials Sector

Key names include Honeywell (HON), a leader in aerospace and smart buildings; UPS, which offers a high dividend yield in the 4% range backed by its monopolistic ground logistics network; and Caterpillar (CAT), which has increased its dividend for over 50 years.

💬 FAQ

Q: Is UPS good to target for high dividends?

A: Yes. Although it is a representative high dividend stock, its margins can fluctuate depending on cargo volume trends and the aftermath of union strikes, requiring periodic monitoring.
