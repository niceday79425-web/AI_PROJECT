Tech Stocks: MSFT, AAPL, AVGO

Although technology stocks currently offer low dividend yields of less than 1%, they are a core asset class that increases dividends by more than 10% annually, yielding immense compounding effects for long-term investors.

💡 Key Point: The massive cash reserves generated from monopolistic platform businesses serve as the source for dividend growth and share buybacks.

1. Low Dividend Yields and the Mechanism of Exponential Growth

While the starting dividend yield is around 1%, dividends grow by 10% to 15% annually, causing the yield on cost to surge remarkably when held for over 10 years.

2. Representative Dividend Powerhouses in the Tech Sector

Representative examples include Microsoft (MSFT), which has grown its dividend annually for over 18 years, and Broadcom (AVGO), which is currently executing exceptional dividend hikes amidst the golden age of AI semiconductors.

💬 FAQ

Q: Is Apple a good dividend stock?

A: Yes. Although its dividend yield itself is low, it executes massive share buybacks and cancellations, making it top-tier from the perspective of total shareholder return.
