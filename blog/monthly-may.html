Hello, this is WiseAIWiseU, your US dividend investing specialist. In May, a popular month for families, a famous adage often circulates in the stock market: "Sell in May and go away." Although this saying originates from past historical experiences where summer returns were statistically lower, for dividend investors, it can be half right and half wrong. This is because high-quality dividend stocks serve as a robust 'shield' when market volatility increases. In today's post, we will explore the unique appeal of dividend stocks that can alleviate concerns about a May downturn, and look at key strategies to implement in the middle of the second quarter.

Key Summary: May's market correction is a 'bargain sale' opportunity to buy high-quality dividend stocks cheap. We must focus on unwavering cash flow rather than capital gains to increase the defensive power of the portfolio.

Detailed Concept Explanation: Sell in May and the Defensive Power of Dividend Stocks

📉 Why Should We Sell in May?

This is based on historical data indicating that stock market returns from May to October were lower than those from November to April. This occurs because trading volumes drop as investors go on vacation, and momentum temporarily enters a lull after Q1 earnings announcements. However, as of 2026, the maturity of the AI industry and the stabilization of interest rates make these simple past statistics irrelevant.

🛡️ The 'Cushion' Effect of Dividend Stocks

When the market declines, dividend stocks protect investors in two ways:

  1. Psychological Support Line: As stock prices fall, the dividend yield relatively increases. This attracts bargain hunters, serving as a strong support line to defend against further stock price declines.
  2. Real Cash Flow: Even if stock prices remain flat or decrease, the promised dividends are deposited. This cash becomes a valuable asset for buying at lows in a down market.

Here are the key stocks to check in May that pay dividends in May or are crucial for the massive quarterly dividends in June.

  • AbbVie (ABBV) & Bristol Myers Squibb (BMY): The healthcare sector has strong defensive characteristics, making it shine during the volatile market conditions in May. In particular, AbbVie pays its dividend in May, giving investors peace of mind.

  • NextEra Energy (NEE): A representative stock of the utility sector, continuing its dividend growth in 2026 as it benefits from the surge in power demand from data centers. It is the first safe haven investors look for when the market shakes.

Practical Implementation: 4 Steps for May Strategy

Step 1: Create a Buy List to Turn 'Fear' into 'Opportunity'

  • Set Watchlist: List dividend stocks (e.g., SCHD, VIG, etc.) that you wanted to buy but found too expensive, and establish price points to enter during market corrections.

Step 2: Prepare for the June 'Dividend Bonanza' in Advance

  • Pre-buying Strategy: Companies on a March/June/September/December cycle pay massive dividends in June. Since you must hold the stock in May to receive the June dividend in many cases, check the ex-dividend dates in advance and complete your purchases.

Step 3: Review 'Family Gift' Dividend Accounts in the Month of Families

  • Future Planning: In celebration of Children's Day in May, open a dividend stock account for your children or grandchildren. Buying promising dividend growth stocks one by one is the best economic education.

Step 4: Leverage WiseAIWiseU Tools

  • Snowball Calculator: Try running a simulation with the assumption, "What if I invest an additional $5,000 during a May downturn?" See for yourself what a difference a larger number of shares secured at lower prices makes in the future.
  • Dividend Scouter: Real-time capture of 'temporarily undervalued' stocks whose dividend yields are at historical highs.

⚠️ Precautions and Risks

  1. Avoid Over-optimism: The idea that 'dividend stocks are always safe' is dangerous. Companies whose earnings decline due to shifts in industry paradigms have no choice but to cut dividends. Be sure to verify the latest earnings.
  2. Potential Delay in Interest Rate Cuts: If the Fed's message turns hawkish, interest-rate-sensitive sectors like REITs and utilities could experience temporary high volatility.
  3. Confirm Tax Settlement: May is the month for comprehensive income tax filing for South Korean investors. If your dividend income exceeded 20 million KRW last year, consult with a tax expert in advance to manage tax risks.

💬 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: Is it really advantageous to sell stocks in May?

A1: It might be true for traders looking for short-term capital gains. However, for long-term investors who reinvest dividends to enjoy compounding, 'Sell in May' can be a mistake where you hand over good stocks to others.

Q2: Which sectors are good to enter in May?

A2: We recommend traditional defensive sectors like consumer staples (food, household goods) and energy/utilities. Considering the artificial intelligence infrastructure demand in 2026, semiconductor equipment stocks that pay dividends are also attractive choices.
