Risk Factors

  • Intensifying Competition: The semiconductor industry is fiercely competitive. While QCOM leads in mobile, rivals like MediaTek and even Apple (with its custom silicon) are significant players. Emerging technologies in AI and edge computing also present new competitive landscapes where QCOM must innovate rapidly to maintain its edge.
  • Geopolitical Tensions & Supply Chain Disruptions: As a global semiconductor company, QCOM is susceptible to trade disputes, export restrictions, and disruptions in the complex global supply chain. Any escalation in geopolitical tensions, particularly involving key manufacturing regions, could impact production and delivery, affecting revenue and profitability.
  • Dependence on Key Customers & Licensing: A significant portion of Qualcomm's revenue comes from its mobile chipset business and its patent licensing (QTL) segment. Reliance on a few major smartphone manufacturers for chip sales and the outcome of ongoing licensing negotiations or disputes can pose a substantial risk. Changes in customer strategies or regulatory scrutiny of licensing practices could negatively impact financial performance.
